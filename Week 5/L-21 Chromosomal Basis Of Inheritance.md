### Chromosomal Basis of Mendel’s Laws

This section connects Gregor Mendel’s theoretical laws of inheritance to the physical movement of chromosomes during cell division.

  * **Law of Segregation**: This law states that the two alleles (different versions of a gene) for each gene separate during gamete formation (the production of sperm or egg cells). Physically, this occurs during **Anaphase I** of meiosis when homologous chromosomes (pairs of chromosomes containing the same genes) separate.
  * **Law of Independent Assortment**: This law explains that alleles of genes on non-homologous chromosomes (chromosomes that are not part of the same pair) assort independently during gamete formation. This means the inheritance of one trait does not affect the inheritance of another, provided the genes are on different chromosomes.
  * **Correlation with Meiosis**: The results of Mendel’s dihybrid crosses (crosses involving two different traits) directly correlate with chromosome behavior in the $F\_{1}$ generation and subsequent random fertilization, leading to the $F\_{2}$ phenotypic ratios Mendel observed.

### Morgan’s Experiment: Genes Associated with Specific Chromosomes

Thomas Hunt Morgan provided the first experimental evidence that chromosomes are the physical locations of Mendel’s heritable factors.

  * **Model System**: Morgan used *Drosophila melanogaster* (the fruit fly) because they are easy to grow, reproduce quickly, and have distinguishable inherited traits.
  * **Nomenclature**: Morgan introduced specific terms to describe fly characteristics:
      * **Wild-type**: The normal phenotype (physical trait) most commonly found in nature, such as red eyes in *Drosophila*. This is denoted with a "+" sign ($w^{+}$).
      * **Mutant**: A phenotype that deviates from the wild-type, such as white eyes.
  * **Key Findings**: Morgan crossed a wild-type red-eyed female with a mutant white-eyed male.
      * The $F\_{1}$ generation all had red eyes, showing the red-eye trait is dominant.
      * The $F\_{2}$ generation showed a 3:1 ratio of red eyes to white eyes, but **only males** exhibited white eyes.
      * **Conclusion**: Morgan concluded the eye-color gene is located specifically on the **X chromosome** and has no corresponding locus (fixed position on a chromosome) on the **Y chromosome**.

### Chromosomal Basis of Sex in Humans

The lecture explains how sex is determined by the inheritance of specific chromosomes.

  * **X-Y System**: Humans have 44 autosomes (non-sex chromosomes) and two sex chromosomes. Females are XX, and males are XY.
  * **Determination of Sex**: The sex of an offspring depends on whether the sperm cell contains an X or a Y chromosome, as the egg always carries an X chromosome.

### Sex-Linked Inheritance

Sex-linked genes are located on either sex chromosome and follow unique inheritance patterns.

  * **Y-linked genes**: Located on the Y chromosome (approximately 78 genes), these mainly determine male sex.
  * **X-linked genes**: Located on the X chromosome (approximately 1,100 genes), these cover many characters unrelated to sex.
  * **Inheritance Patterns**:
      * Fathers pass X-linked alleles to all daughters but none of their sons.
      * Mothers can pass X-linked alleles to both sons and daughters.
      * Males are more likely to express X-linked recessive disorders because they only have one X chromosome; any recessive allele inherited from the mother will be expressed.

### Examples of Sex-Linked Disorders

The document highlights several conditions caused by X-linked recessive traits.

  * **Red-Green Color Blindness**: A common X-linked disorder where individuals cannot distinguish between certain colors. A female is a **carrier** (has the gene but doesn't show the trait) if she has one normal allele and one recessive allele ($X^{N}X^{n}$).
  * **Hemophilia**: A disorder characterized by the absence of proteins required for blood clotting, leading to excessive bleeding.
      * **Historical Context**: Hemophilia was prominent in the Royal Families of Europe, famously passed down by Queen Victoria of England.
      * **Modern Treatment**: Today, hemophilia is treated with intravenous injections of the missing purified clotting proteins.
  * **Other Disorders**: These include Duchenne muscular dystrophy and night blindness.
